How to manage induction, training and support for early career teachers (ECTs)

Information on setting up and managing the 2-year induction for ECTs, and the DfE-funded training based on the early career framework (ECF) that’s now part of statutory induction.

From: Department for Education

Published 24 October 2023

Last updated 24 October 2023

Applies to England

Choose or change the registered induction tutor for your school

Induction tutors monitor and support your ECTs. They conduct:

  • regular progress reviews
  • 2 formal assessment meetings with ECTs during the 2-year induction period - one midway through and one at the end

Read about the roles and responsibilities of induction tutors in section 5 of the statutory guidance on induction for ECTs.

Your school may have several people performing this role, but only one can be registered to report information to the Department for Education (DfE) using the Manage training for early career teachers service.

Find out how to report information to the DfE.

Who to nominate

Choose someone who:

  • holds qualified teacher status (QTS)
  • can assess an ECT’s progress against the Teachers’ Standards

Schools commonly choose someone in a senior leadership position, such as an assistant head.

Try to choose someone who is not also a mentor. The 2 roles have separate and different responsibilities.

Nominate your first registered induction tutor

Use the Manage training for early career teachers service to do this.

DfE will send an access link to the email address your school provided to the Get Information About Schools (GIAS) register.

Change your current registered induction tutor

If your current induction tutor is leaving your school, or will no longer be acting as your induction tutor, they should follow these steps.

  1. Sign into the Manage training for early career teachers service using their registered email address.
  2. Select the ‘Change’ link next to their name.
  3. Enter the name and email address of the person who will report information to the DfE instead.

If your registered induction tutor has left your school, or cannot access the service

If no one at your school can access the Manage training for early career teachers service to change your registered induction tutor, you can request an access link for your school.

Choose or change how to provide ECF-based training at your school

ECTs are entitled to 2 years of training based on the early career framework (ECF).

Option 1: use a lead provider

Lead providers work with delivery partners such as trusts, teaching school hubs and universities to deliver training directly to your ECTs and their mentors.

This is fully funded by the DfE, so there’s no cost for eligible schools. Learn about funding for training based on the early career framework.

Providers may need to use face to face sessions as part of their training, so contact them to check where they’re based before signing up.

To sign up with a lead provider, contact them:

  • Ambition Institute
  • Best Practice Network (home of Outstanding Leaders Partnership)
  • Education Development Trust
  • National Institute of Teaching, founded by the School-Led Development Trust
  • Teach First
  • UCL Institute of Education

If you choose to use a lead provider, your registered induction tutor will need to report this through the Manage training for early career teachers service each academic year. They do not need to tell us which provider your school will work with. Your lead provider is responsible for reporting this to us directly.

Option 2: deliver your own training using DfE-accredited materials

DfE-accredited materials include mentor session guidelines and self-directed study materials.

Create a Support for early career teachers account to see what materials are available.

You can choose materials from one of the following suppliers:

  • Ambition Institute
  • Education Development Trust
  • Teach First
  • UCL Early Career Teacher Consortium

If you choose to deliver your own training using DfE-accredited materials, your registered induction tutor will need to report this through the Manage training for early career teachers service.

Your appropriate body will check your programme to make sure it covers the full depth of the early career framework.

Option 3: design and deliver your own training

You can design and deliver your own 2-year training programme covering every ‘learn that’ and ‘learn how to’ statement in the early career framework.

You can use DfE-accredited training materials for reference. These include mentor session guidelines and self-directed study materials.

Create a Support for early career teachers account to see what materials are available.

Talk to your appropriate body for advice on next steps.

Your appropriate body will check your programme to make sure it covers the early career framework.

Avoid changing your training option partway through induction.

Appoint an appropriate body

ECTs cannot start their induction until you’ve appointed an appropriate body.

What an appropriate body does

Appropriate bodies quality assure the induction of ECTs. For example, they:

  • check that your ECTs receive their statutory entitlements, such as mentoring throughout their 2-year induction period
  • make sure formal assessments conducted by your induction tutor are fair and appropriate

Unless you use a training provider to deliver your training, your appropriate body will also check that your training covers the full depth of the .

Your appropriate body can also give advice if your ECTs are serving a reduced or part time induction. They’ll need to agree to any reduction to the induction period.

You must provide each ECT with contact information for their appropriate body.

Find an appropriate body

Find an appropriate body and contact them to appoint them.

Who you can and cannot appoint

You can appoint one appropriate body for all of your ECTs, but you may need or choose to appoint different ones.

You may have previously used a local authority as an appropriate body. From September 2023, you cannot use a local authority for new inductions, so you’ll need to choose another appropriate body for any new ECTs.

If an ECT did their initial teacher training (ITT) through an accredited ITT provider who is also an appropriate body, you cannot appoint that appropriate body for that teacher.

ECTs may be serving their induction at an appropriate body. For example, they may work at a teaching school hub that is also an appropriate body. If this is the case, you cannot appoint that appropriate body for that teacher.

Learn more about who you can and cannot appoint in section 2 of the statutory guidance on induction for early career teachers.

Arrange mentoring

ECTs are entitled to mentor support throughout their 2-year induction period.

Mentors will only be eligible for funded training and time off timetable once they’re assigned to an ECT in the DfE service. Find out how to register and assign mentors.

What mentors do

Mentors make sure that your ECTs receive a high quality induction. They:

  • meet regularly with ECTs to provide support and feedback
  • take prompt, appropriate action if their ECT is experiencing difficulties

If you use a provider to deliver your training, mentors may also be asked to observe your ECTs. This is for professional development purposes and must not be used for formal assessment.

Read about the role and responsibilities of mentors in section 5 of the statutory guidance on induction for early career teachers.

Share the guidance for mentors to help them understand their role.

Who to choose as a mentor

Choose mentors who have the skills to provide mentoring for specific phases and subject areas. This will normally be someone who has qualified teacher status (QTS).

If you choose someone who does not have qualified teacher status, you should check this decision with your appropriate body.

Try to choose someone who is not also an induction tutor for your school. The 2 roles have separate and different responsibilities.

Manage mentor time

Timetable mentor sessions during teaching hours wherever possible. Make sure your mentors have enough time to meet with their ECTs regularly.

Schools receive funding to cover mentor time off timetable.

Mentor training

If you use a training provider to deliver training for your ECTs, mentors will be given 36 hours of training over 2 years.

Schools receive funding for 36 hours of mentor time off timetable if mentors attend this training. Learn more about funding.

Mentors can only do this training and receive funded time off timetable for this training once. However, they can keep being a mentor as many times as they like.

Mentors can continue their training even if their ECT leaves your school or withdraws part way through their induction.

Mentor concerns

If a mentor has concerns or difficulties, they should contact your school’s induction tutor.

If a mentor does not have the time or ability to carry out their role effectively, talk to your appropriate body.

Report your training information to the DfE

Use the Manage training for early career teachers service to:

  • check if ECTs are eligible to serve statutory induction, and receive DfE funding for training and support based on the early career framework (ECF)
  • check if mentors working with ECTs are eligible for DfE funding for ECF-based training and time off timetable
  • get access to ECF-based training materials for ECTs and their mentors

Schools in England must use this service to:

  • nominate one person as their registered user (or ’induction tutor’)
  • report how they’ll provide ECF-based training for ECTs each academic year
  • register each ECT and their mentor
  • report which mentor each ECT will work with to ensure the correct funding is paid
  • report if a registered ECT or mentor transfers to another school in England during their ECF-based induction or training

Your school will need to provide some of this information to your appropriate body and training provider too, if you work with one.

Start now

If you have problems accessing or using the service, email continuing-professional-development@digital.education.gov.uk for help.

Register ECTs and mentors

ECTs and mentors taking part in ECF-based training must be registered with the DfE if your school:

  • uses a training provider funded by the DfE
  • delivers your own training using DfE-accredited materials

We need this information to confirm their eligibility for funding, and arrange access to training and materials.

Your school does not need to register ECTs and mentors with the DfE if you choose to:

  • design and deliver your own training
  • work with a training provider funded directly by your school (if your school is not eligible for DfE-funded training, for example)

How to register an ECT or mentor with the DfE

Your school’s registered induction tutor must sign in to the Manage training for early career teachers service to register each ECT and mentor.

You’ll be asked to enter their:

  • full name
  • teacher reference number
  • date of birth
  • email address (personal or school)
  • start date at your school, if they’re moving from another school

If your school does not have this information, contact the person directly to ask for it.

Assign each ECT a mentor

Your school’s registered induction tutor must also use the Manage training for early career teachers service to tell us which mentor will work with each ECT. This is an important step to make sure that relevant funding is paid.

Find out how mentoring ECTs works.

Manage statutory induction and training for ECTs

Throughout statutory induction you must:

  • arrange for each ECT’s teaching to be observed at regular intervals
  • make sure ECTs and mentors have adequate time off timetable to participate fully in ECF-based training and mentoring
  • following any other instructions from your appropriate body or training provider

In terms 1, 2, 4 and 5 of a standard 2-year induction, you must arrange progress reviews against the Teachers’ Standards for each ECT.

In terms 3 and 6 of a standard 2-year induction, you must arrange for each ECT to be formally assessed by your school’s induction tutor or headteacher.

See section 2 of the statutory guidance for information on how to complete these tasks.

Adapting training and induction for ECTs working part time, or completing a reduced induction

If any ECTs are serving a reduced or part-time induction at your school, your headteacher and appropriate body should decide on the best ECF-based training, progress review and assessment schedule for them. This must also be shared with your training provider if you work with one.

See guidance on proportionate funding for these ECTs.

For ECTs completing their induction and ECF-based training

Follow:

  • your appropriate body’s instructions to submit a final assessment for each ECT
  • any other instructions from your appropriate body or training provider

You do not need to withdraw or delete their information from the DfE service. Your training provider and appropriate body will confirm that they’ve completed training and induction, and we’ll update their records for you.

For ECTs starting their induction and ECF-based training in the next academic year

You must:

  • appoint an appropriate body for each ECT
  • if you want to work with a training provider, contact them to confirm their availability for the next academic year

We'll contact your school in the summer term when it’s time to sign in to the DfE service and report your training information for the next academic year.

Report if something changes for an ECT or mentor

Your school’s registered induction tutor should sign in to the DfE service to report if:

  • a new ECT or mentor begins their induction or ECF-based training part-way through the year
  • an ECT or mentor transfers to your school, or moves to another school, part-way through their induction or ECF-based training
  • you no longer expect any ECTs to start their statutory induction at your school that year
  • the headteacher wants someone else to be registered as your school’s induction tutor instead (it’s important to do this before you leave your role or the school)
  • an ECT or mentor’s name or email address changes

If your school works with a training provider

Contact them directly to:

  • tell them you want to work with a different training provider instead (they’ll report this to us)
  • withdraw or defer an ECT or mentor who has left teaching, is taking a break, or takes a teaching job outside of England (for ECTs, you must report this to their appropriate body too)
  • you no longer expect any ECTs to start their statutory induction at your school that year
  • reinstate an ECT or mentor who was previously withdrawn from training

When to contact the DfE support team

Email us if you want to change your:

  • ECF-based training option
  • DfE-accredited training materials

Email: continuing-professional-development@digital.education.gov.uk
